About Udaipur



Udaipur City Palace


🏰 Udaipur City Palace – The Royal Gem of Mewar
📍 Location Stands majestically on the eastern bank of Lake Pichola.
🕰️ History Construction started in 1559 AD by Maharana Udai Singh II, the founder of Udaipur.
Expanded over time by successive rulers of the Mewar dynasty.
🏗️ Architecture

  • A blend of Rajasthani and Mughal styles.
  • Built with granite and marble, adorned with balconies, towers, and domes.
  • ✨ Major Attractions
  • Mor Chowk (Peacock Courtyard) – Famous for colorful glass mosaic peacocks.
  • Sheesh Mahal – Decorated with mirrors and glasswork.
  • rishna Vilas – Houses miniature paintings and royal artworks.
  • City Palace Museum – Displays royal weapons, costumes, and artifacts.

Lake Pichola


🏞️ Lake Pichola – The Heart of Udaipur
📍 Location Situated in the city of Udaipur, Rajasthan, India.
🕰️ History Built in 1362 AD by Pichhu Banjara during the reign of Maharana Lakha. Named after the nearby Picholi Village.
🌅 Key Highlights Artificial freshwater lake surrounded by hills, palaces, and temples.

    Dimensions: 4 km long and 3 km wide (approx). Famous for sunset boat rides and scenic views.
    Four major islands:
    • Jag Niwas – Home to the Lake Palace Hotel.
    • Jag Mandir – Known for its beautiful marble architecture.
    • Mohan Mandir – Used by royals to watch processions.
    • Arsi Vilas – A small island and bird sanctuary.
